This Privacy Policy explains how What Should I Say? handles information when you use the app.

Information the app stores

The app allows users to generate, edit, save, and share prewritten supportive messages. Saved favorites may be stored locally on your device using Apple technologies such as SwiftData.

Information collection

  • The app does not require account creation.
  • The app does not sell personal information.
  • The app does not intentionally collect personally identifiable information through a user account system.
  • The app does not use third-party advertising SDKs.

Analytics and tracking

At this time, the app is not designed to track users across apps or websites. If analytics tools are added in the future, this Privacy Policy should be updated before release.

Data sharing

Information is not shared with third parties except where required by law, to protect rights and safety, or as necessary for core platform functionality provided by Apple.

Local storage

Any messages you choose to save as favorites may remain stored locally on your device unless you delete the app or remove that saved content.

Children's privacy

The app is not directed to children under 13, and it is not intended to knowingly collect personal information from children.

Your choices

  • You can choose not to save favorites.
  • You can remove saved content from within the app.
  • You can stop using the app at any time.

Changes to this policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. When updates are made, the revised version should include a new effective date.
